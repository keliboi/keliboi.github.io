Developed with Java and JavaFX using widgets, events, layout, and Model-View-Controller architecture. Designed and built a simple user interface using the iterative design process including task descriptions, sketch-based prototypes, screen design, and task walkthroughs.

Bahay Kubo

A black-and-white still life photography project.


"The choice of subject matter is based on “Bahay Kubo”, a traditional Filipino folk song, which is lyrically about valuing the abundance of vegetables and crops, while living a humble life in a stilted hut, which is a reminder that wealth is not just about money, but also many things that we easily over appreciate. With such subject matter, still life is a seamless way to purely emphasize any inanimate objects around us. This series makes use of “chiaroscuro”. Exposure to the works of Vermeer, Rembrandt, and Goya, from my art history class, has made me a fan of such lighting technique, as the play with the highlights and shadows can bring very significant styling effects to any visual art."

Worm

A stop motion animation project.


This short clip is heavily inspired by Elise Fachon's PIN, in which they were able to give life and animate a simple object such as a pin. Frames for this clip were shot using a mirrorless camera. By using a wire, which is visible in some frames, I was able to animate a piece of string to crawl like a caterpillar, which crawls forward its first half of its body, then quickly drags its back part of its body. The mid-scene is added to give such caterpillar a playful personality which is very common in animation.
