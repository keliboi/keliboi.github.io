Hello, how are you?

My name is Kelly Neuda. I have recently graduated from the University of Saskatchewan with an Interactive Systems Design degree, which combines courses in art and art history, psychology, and computer science to provide knowledge and skills in designing, building, and evaluating interactive systems. I was part of several group projects from different courses, where I had hands-on experience in front-end requirement analysis, prototyping, and implementation of graphical user interfaces.

I interned as a Full Stack Developer Student at Federated Co-operatives Limited in Saskatoon, where I assisted in developing web-applications to improve business processes for stakeholders both internally and externally. In said internship, I had extensive experience in identifying and resolving software issues to improve functionality and performance, enhancing user experience and efficiency. Such role also refined my proficiency in front-end web technologies such as JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and jQuery. I was also able to work on updating RESTful APIs and databases to interact with new web-applications.

I have years of experience in finding customer-focused solutions, and values collaborative communication from my leadership experiences in customer service. I have the enthusiasm for learning and sharing experiences with colleagues, and I am excited to take on challenges and grow in such dynamic field.

Worm

A stop motion animation project.

This short clip is heavily inspired by Elise Fachon's PIN, in which they were able to give life and animate a simple object such as a pin. Frames for this clip were shot using a mirrorless camera. By using a wire, which is visible in some frames, I was able to animate a piece of string to crawl like a caterpillar, which crawls forward its first half of its body, then quickly drags its back part of its body. The mid-scene is added to give such caterpillar a playful personality which is very common in animation.
